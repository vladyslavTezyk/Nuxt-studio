# Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji (富士山, *Fujisan*) is the highest and most iconic mountain in Japan, standing at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet). Located on Honshu Island, about 100 kilometers southwest of Tokyo, it is an active stratovolcano and a symbol of Japanese culture, art, and spirituality.

## Geography

- **Elevation:** 3,776 meters (12,389 feet)
- **Location:** Honshu Island, Japan
- **Type:** Stratovolcano (active, last erupted in 1707–1708)
- **Coordinates:** 35°21′29″N 138°43′52″E

## Notable Features

- **Symmetrical Cone:** Mount Fuji is renowned for its nearly perfect symmetrical cone, which is snow-capped for several months each year.
- **Five Lakes:** The mountain is surrounded by five beautiful lakes—Kawaguchi, Yamanaka, Sai, Motosu, and Shoji.
- **UNESCO World Heritage Site:** In 2013, Mount Fuji was designated as a UNESCO World Cultural Heritage Site for its cultural significance.

## Cultural and Historical Significance

Mount Fuji has been a sacred site in Shinto and Buddhist traditions for centuries. It is featured in countless works of art, including the famous woodblock prints by Hokusai and Hiroshige. The mountain is also a popular subject in poetry and literature, symbolizing beauty, endurance, and national identity.

## Outdoor Activities

- **Climbing:** The official climbing season is from early July to early September. Thousands of climbers ascend Mount Fuji each year via four main routes.
- **Hiking and Nature Walks:** The surrounding Fuji Five Lakes region offers scenic trails and views of the mountain.
- **Photography:** Mount Fuji is a favorite subject for photographers, especially during cherry blossom season and sunrise (known as *Goraiko*).
