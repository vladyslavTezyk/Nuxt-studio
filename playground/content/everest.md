# Mount Everest

Mount Everest (सगरमाथा *Sagarmāthā* in Nepali, *Chomolungma* in Tibetan) is the highest mountain on Earth, standing at 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level. Located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China, Everest is a symbol of adventure, endurance, and the ultimate mountaineering challenge.

## Geography

- **Elevation:** 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet)
- **Location:** Border of Nepal and Tibet (China)
- **Coordinates:** 27°59′17″N 86°55′31″E
- **Range:** Himalayas

## Notable Features

- **Highest Point on Earth:** Everest's summit is the highest point above global mean sea level.
- **The "Death Zone":** Above 8,000 meters, the air is so thin that climbers require supplemental oxygen and face extreme risks.
- **Khumbu Icefall:** A dangerous and ever-shifting section of the South Col route, known for its deep crevasses and moving ice.
- **Base Camps:** Two main base camps—South Base Camp in Nepal (5,364 m) and North Base Camp in Tibet (5,150 m)—serve as starting points for expeditions.

## Climbing History

- **First Ascent:** Sir Edmund Hillary (New Zealand) and Tenzing Norgay (Nepal) reached the summit on May 29, 1953.
- **Climbing Routes:** The two main routes are the Southeast Ridge from Nepal and the North Ridge from Tibet.
- **Climbing Season:** The best time to climb is during the pre-monsoon (April–May) and post-monsoon (September–October) periods.
- **Challenges:** Climbers face extreme cold, high winds, avalanches, altitude sickness, and crowded routes.

## Cultural and Historical Significance

Mount Everest holds deep spiritual significance for local Sherpa people and Tibetans, who regard it as sacred. The mountain has inspired countless stories, documentaries, and research into human endurance and the effects of high altitude.

## Outdoor Activities

- **Mountaineering:** Everest is the ultimate goal for many climbers, though it requires significant experience, preparation, and resources.
- **Trekking:** The trek to Everest Base Camp in Nepal is one of the world's most popular and scenic trekking routes.
- **Photography:** The region offers breathtaking views of Everest and neighboring peaks like Lhotse and Nuptse.
