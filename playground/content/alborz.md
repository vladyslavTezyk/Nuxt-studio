# Mount Alborz

The **Alborz Mountain** (also spelled Elburz or Alburz) is a majestic mountain range in northern Iran, stretching from the borders of Azerbaijan along the western and entire southern coast of the Caspian Sea, and ending in the Khorasan region in the northeast.

## Geography

- **Length:** Approximately 900 kilometers (560 miles)
- **Highest Peak:** Mount Damavand (5,610 meters / 18,406 feet), which is also the highest peak in Iran and the Middle East.
- **Location:** Separates the Caspian Sea from the Iranian plateau.

## Notable Features

- **Mount Damavand:** A potentially active stratovolcano and a prominent symbol in Persian mythology and literature.
- **Diverse Climate:** The northern slopes are lush and forested due to the Caspian Sea's moisture, while the southern slopes are arid.
- **Biodiversity:** Home to unique flora and fauna, including forests of beech, oak, and hornbeam.

## Cultural and Historical Significance

The Alborz range has played a significant role in Persian history and mythology. It is mentioned in many ancient texts and is considered a sacred mountain in Zoroastrianism.

## Outdoor Activities

- **Hiking and Trekking:** Popular destination for mountaineers, especially those aiming to summit Mount Damavand.
- **Skiing:** Several ski resorts, such as Dizin and Shemshak, are located in the Alborz mountains.
- **Nature Tours:** The region offers beautiful landscapes, waterfalls, and wildlife observation opportunities.
